MECO Engineering, together with its Architectural Branch (MECO A/E), provided
comprehensive MEP design services for the new construction of The Crossing in
Quincy, Illinois. Working closely with the owner of the project and the City of Quincy,
MECO ensured full compliance with all local ordinances while delivering a functional
facility that met the organization’s needs. the team designed all the MEP
components of the new building, including all interior and exterior lighting, HVAC
systems, and complete bathroom plumbing. This project highlights MECO’s
capability to deliver integrated, code-compliant MEP solution for modern, new
developments.

===== PAGE 10 =====
MECO worked closely with the City of Hunnewell, MO to provide comprehensive services to
upgrade the city’s electric distribution system, a project driven by the city’s need for system
improvements. MECO assisted the city in securing the necessary funding and then utilizing
these funds to deliver critical upgrades to the medium-voltage distribution system.
Improvements included the installation of 50 new poles equipped with modern technology,
5,000 feet of durable ACSR conductors, underground service feeds to residences, 11 new
pole transformers guided by detailed load calculations, and additional enhancements to
strengthen overall system reliability. This project demonstrates MECO’s expertise in
medium-voltage distribution as well as its commitment to working with client budgets,
ensuring essential needs are met while providing informed, cost-effective
recommendations.

There are many ways to measure past performance and the standards are different for each. However,
we firmly hold that our actions that result in client satisfaction and ultimately, client retention, are the
best measure of success. Our goal is to provide a quality project, on time and on budget.
Control of Costs
MECO estimates the project costs based on historical bids in cooperation with the current industry
rates. Once the project has been awarded, we track each of the contracts under construction and the
allocated amount awarded. Monthly pay applications submitted are reviewed and approved. Should
change orders be necessary, they are incorporated within the excel sheet of each contract where
funds are taken from the contingencies to increase their specific area contracts. We follow the project
from conceptual project drawings and estimates through preliminary engineering reports, financing
document submittals, the bidding process, and final construction costs. Final lean waivers for material
suppliers and affidavits for prevailing wages are obtained to ensure all is in order prior to final payment
and completion of projects. This process allows MECO to keep a close eye on cost and keep the
county fully informed of the progress of the project. 
Quality Control/ Quality Assurance
Our Quality Control/Quality Assurance Team is comprised of principals/senior engineers who bring
their years of combined experience and knowledge to the review table.
